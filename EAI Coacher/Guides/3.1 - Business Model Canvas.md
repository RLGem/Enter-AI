### 2.2 Filling Each Block (With Real Data)

| Block | What to Write | Common Mistake |
|-------|-------------|----------------|
| **Customer Segments** | Specific persona. "Freelance designers in the US, 1-5 clients, billing $3K-10K/month" | "Everyone" or "small businesses" |
| **Value Proposition** | The ONE thing that makes them choose you. From 3.2. | Listing features instead of outcomes |
| **Channels** | The actual channels driving customers today. From 4.1. | Listing channels you *wish* worked |
| **Customer Relationships** | Self-service? Personal? Community? How do you retain them? | Ignoring this block |
| **Revenue Streams** | Subscription, one-time, usage-based. Actual prices. | Vague "premium plan" without numbers |
| **Key Resources** | Your tech stack, your time, your expertise, your community | Overcomplicating â€” you're a solo founder |
| **Key Activities** | Product development, customer support, marketing. The 3-5 things you do weekly. | Listing 20 activities (you can't do 20 things) |
| **Key Partners** | Payment processors, hosting, API providers, distribution partners | Listing "dream partners" not real ones |
| **Cost Structure** | Monthly costs: tools, hosting, marketing, your salary draw | Forgetting to include your own compensation |

### 2.3 The Data-Driven BMC

**Rule:** Every block should contain at least ONE number.

### 2.4 Finding Weaknesses

After filling the BMC, scan for these common problems:

| Weakness | Signal | Fix |
|----------|--------|-----|
| **Single channel dependency** | 80%+ customers from one channel | Add a second channel. See 4.1. |
| **No retention mechanism** | No onboarding, no email, no community | Build one retention loop (email drip, weekly summary, community) |
| **Revenue doesn't cover costs** | Costs > Revenue even at full capacity | Raise prices (4.2) or cut costs |
| **Key person risk** | Everything depends on you | Document processes (5.2), start delegating (5.3) |
| **No moat** | Anyone could copy you tomorrow | Build: data advantage, community, brand, switching costs |

## 3. DECISION TABLES

### BMC health check:

| Question | Healthy Answer | Warning Sign |
|----------|---------------|--------------|
| Can you fill every block with real data? | Yes, using actual numbers | You're guessing on 3+ blocks |
| Is revenue > costs? | Yes, or clear path to it | Revenue < 50% of costs |
| Do you have 2+ channels? | Yes | Single channel = single point of failure |
| Is your value proposition in their words? | Yes (from customer interviews) | It's in your words (feature-speak) |
| Can someone else do your key activities? | At least some of them | Only you can do everything |

### When to redo the BMC:

| Trigger | Action |
|---------|--------|
| Pivot to new audience | Redo Customer Segments, Channels, Value Proposition |
| New pricing model | Redo Revenue Streams, Cost Structure |
| Lost major channel | Redo Channels, find alternatives |
| Quarterly review | Update all blocks with latest data |

## 5. RED FLAGS

- **Founder fills BMC with aspirations, not facts** â†’ "Every block should have a real number. If you write 'influencer partnerships' under Channels but have zero partnerships â€” that's a wish, not a business model. Fill it with what IS, not what you hope."
- **Founder's Value Proposition is a feature list** â†’ "You wrote '5 features.' Your customers don't care about features. They care about 'saves me 3 hours every week.' Rewrite it in their words."
- **Revenue doesn't cover costs** â†’ "Your BMC shows $2,000/month in costs and $1,200/month in revenue. That's not a business model â€” it's a countdown to zero. What changes: raise price, cut costs, or get more customers?"
- **Founder skips the BMC** â†’ "You're running a business without a map. The BMC takes 30 minutes to fill out. It will show you exactly where your business is strong and where it's vulnerable."
