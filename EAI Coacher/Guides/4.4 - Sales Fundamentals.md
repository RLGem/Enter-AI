### 2.4 Handling Objections

| Objection | What They Really Mean | Response |
|-----------|----------------------|----------|
| "Too expensive" | Value not clear enough | "I understand. Let me ask: how much time/money does [problem] cost you now? If this saves you [X], would $[price] make sense?" |
| "I need to think about it" | Need more information or reassurance | "Totally fair. What specific questions can I answer to help you decide?" |
| "I'll try it later" | Not urgent enough | "What would need to happen for this to become urgent? Is there a deadline or event coming up where this would help?" |
| "Does it do [feature]?" | Checking if it fits their specific need | Be honest. "Yes, it does" or "Not yet â€” that's on our roadmap. But the core [X] works great today." |
| "I tried something similar and it didn't work" | Trust issue | "What didn't work about it? [Listen] I built this specifically to avoid that problem. Here's how..." |
| "Can I get a discount?" | Testing your confidence | "The price reflects the value. But I can offer: annual plan at 20% off, or a 7-day free trial to prove it works for you." |

### 2.5 Closing Techniques for Non-Salespeople

| Technique | Script | When to Use |
|-----------|--------|-------------|
| **Direct Ask** | "Want to get started?" | When they're clearly interested |
| **Assumptive** | "Should I set up your account now?" | After a positive demo |
| **Trial Close** | "If this solved [specific problem], would it be worth $39/mo?" | When they're hesitating |
| **Urgency** | "I'm onboarding 5 beta customers this week at a launch price" | When they need a nudge |
| **Risk Reversal** | "Try it for 30 days. If it doesn't help, full refund." | When they're risk-averse |

### 2.6 Sales Channels for Solo Founders

| Channel | Conversion Rate | Effort | Best For |
|---------|----------------|--------|----------|
| **1-on-1 conversations** (DM, call, email) | 10-30% | High per sale | First 10 customers |
| **Email to waitlist** | 3-10% | Low per sale | Scaling past personal reach |
| **Social media post** | 1-3% | Medium | Building awareness |
| **Cold outreach** (LinkedIn, email) | 1-5% | High volume needed | B2B, services |
| **Community posts** (Reddit, FB groups) | 1-3% | Medium | Niche products |

**First 5 customers: Always 1-on-1.** Don't try to scale sales before you've done it personally 5 times.

## 3. DECISION TABLES

### Sales approach by business type:

| Type | Primary Channel | Script Focus |
|------|----------------|-------------|
| SaaS / digital product | Email + demo call | Show the product working for their use case |
| Service business | 1-on-1 call or meeting | Focus on their problem, then present your package |
| Digital product (one-time) | Email sequence + LP | Urgency + social proof + guarantee |
| B2B product | LinkedIn + call | ROI calculation + case study |

### When to push vs. back off:

| Signal | Action |
|--------|--------|
| "This is exactly what I need" | Close NOW. Don't add more info. "Great â€” let me send you the link." |
| Asking detailed questions about features | They're interested. Answer, then close. "Does that answer your question? Ready to try it?" |
| "Sounds interesting" (no energy) | They're being polite. Ask: "Is this something you'd actually use this week?" |
| Not responding to follow-up | Send ONE more follow-up after 5 days. Then stop. Chasing kills relationships. |
| "I need to ask my [partner/boss]" | Offer to talk to them together. "Would it help if I joined a quick call with them?" |
